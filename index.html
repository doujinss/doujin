What is doujin

The term "doujin"b> refers to self-published works, usually in the form of comics, novels, or fan-made content, created by amateur or independent creators in Japan. The word "doujinshi" is often used to describe self-published fan-made comics that are based on existing manga, anime, video games, or other media. Doujinshi can range from original stories to parodies or adaptations of existing works. Creators produce doujinshi for a variety of reasons, including personal expression, creative exploration, and sharing their love for a particular series or genre. While doujinshi can cover a wide array of topics and themes, they are often associated with fan communities and events like Comiket (Comic Market), one of the largest doujinshi fairs held in Tokyo. It's important to note that the term "doujin" itself doesn't exclusively refer to fan-created works. It can also encompass original creations made by independent creators who want to publish their content outside of mainstream commercial channels.
